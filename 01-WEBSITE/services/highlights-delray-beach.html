Highlights vs. Balayage: Which Is Right for You?

Foil highlights use foils to isolate strands and create precise, consistent lightening from root to tip. They offer more control over placement and can achieve lighter, brighter results than balayage.

Best for: Those wanting maximum brightness, more defined contrast, or seamless gray coverage. Foils are also ideal for correcting uneven color or achieving platinum/silver tones.

As a certified Davines educator and Goldwell Academy graduate, Chris David can help you decide which technique - or combination - will achieve your dream hair.

Choose Highlights If:

  • • You want maximum brightness
  • • You need gray coverage
  • • You prefer defined contrast

Choose Balayage If:

  • • You want low maintenance
  • • You prefer soft, natural blend
  • • You like root-to-tip gradient
