What is Balayage?

Balayage (French for "sweeping") is a freehand highlighting technique where color is hand-painted onto the hair, creating a natural, sun-kissed effect that traditional foil highlights simply can't achieve.

Unlike regular highlights, balayage creates a softer, more natural graduation of color - lighter at the ends, gradually blending into your natural color at the roots. This means low-maintenance grow-out and a look that stays beautiful for months.

As a certified Davines educator and Goldwell Academy graduate, Chris David has spent over 20 years perfecting his balayage technique. His signature "air composition color mapping" creates dimension and movement that's uniquely tailored to your features.

Balayage FAQs

How long does balayage last?

Balayage typically lasts 3-4 months between appointments, though many clients go even longer. Because the color is hand-painted without harsh lines, it grows out naturally and beautifully.

Is balayage damaging to hair?

When done correctly by an experienced colorist using quality products like Davines, balayage is actually gentler than traditional highlights. Chris uses bond-building treatments to maintain hair health.

What's the difference between balayage and highlights?

Traditional highlights use foils and create uniform, consistent lightening. Balayage is hand-painted in a sweeping motion, creating softer, more natural-looking dimension with no harsh regrowth lines.

Can I get balayage on dark hair?

Absolutely! Balayage looks stunning on all hair colors. For dark hair, Chris creates rich caramel, honey, or copper tones that add beautiful dimension while maintaining your natural base.

How do I maintain my balayage?

Use color-safe, sulfate-free shampoo (Chris recommends Davines products), avoid excessive heat styling, and protect your hair from sun exposure. A gloss treatment every 6-8 weeks keeps color vibrant.
